Great question! Eevee is Blender's real-time render engine. It's very fast, like a video game engine, and is great for previews, stylized looks, and game assets. Cycles is a path-tracing render engine that's more physically accurate. It simulates light bounces to produce more realistic lighting, shadows, and reflections, but it generally takes longer to render. Many artists use Eevee for look development and then switch to Cycles for final high-quality renders.
